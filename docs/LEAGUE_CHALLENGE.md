# League Challenge

The full gym circuit, Elite Four, and Champion for Pokémon: Wet-Hot. Locations and dual-type themes are fixed; rosters are final; levels are governed by the scaling rules below.

---

## Structure

- **Eight gyms, any order.** The player picks their own route across the region.
- **Difficulty scales to badges held**, so the first gym attempted is always the easiest and the eighth is always the hardest, whichever physical gym it is.
- **Team Paradise finale** triggers at 7 to 8 badges, after the last gym and before the Elite Four, climaxing at Paradise Island off Key West.
- **Elite Four** sit at the center of Lake Okeechobee and are fought as **double battles**.
- **Champion** closes the run.
- Badge names: TBD.

---

## Difficulty Scaling

**Ace level = 13 + (7 x badges held).** Ace species is fixed per leader; only level and team size scale.

| Badges held | Ace level | Team size | Support levels |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 (first gym) | 13 | 3 | ace -2 to -4 |
| 1 | 20 | 3 | ace -2 to -4 |
| 2 | 27 | 4 | ace -2 to -4 |
| 3 | 34 | 4 | ace -2 to -4 |
| 4 | 41 | 5 | ace -2 to -3 |
| 5 | 48 | 5 | ace -2 to -3 |
| 6 | 55 | 6 | ace -1 to -3 |
| 7 (eighth gym) | 62 | 6 | ace -1 to -3 |

Leaders may also gain better held items and an extra coverage move at higher badge counts.

post-gym levels: Elite Four aces roughly 68, 70, 72, 74; Champion ace roughly 76.
